Vikram Murugan took third place in his middle school art competition for drawing a perspective skyline for a futuristic prompt. He posts his drawings and sketches online (@vikram_murugan2003) . Outside of art, he has received many awards for mechanical engineering and design. He has been the hardware lead on a highschool robotics team that has gone to world championships. He volunteers teaching special education students coding and writes curriculum to make STEM more accessible.

Vikram is a first generation Indian American. His grandfather immigrated to the United States and was able to secure a job, bringing his family to settle into Newark California. His mother went to Berkley and after began to work in big tech. His father grew up in rural India and came over to the United States after marriage. There they raised Vikram with his grandparents for several years, before moving out to Fremont as his brother was born. Vikram had been constantly building with legos and drawing since he was 6.

Vikram spends lots of time learning. When he is not studying, he leads his robotics team to compete and design robots, mentoring new members to ensure a future for the team. He loves reading webcomics, watching anime, cooking dishes, listening to music, and playing mobile games in his free time.
