Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood

Fullmetal Alchemist originally started as a manga before being turned into two separate animes: Fullmetal Alchemist (2003-2004) and Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood (2009-2012). It had also been turned into a live action movie in 2017 by Netflix.

Art Style

Edward Elric has two main styles. He has a very realistic drawing style with reasonable proportions with the exception of stylized eyes. He has “anime” eyes meaning they are far larger than realistic eyes, however it isn’t excessive. In the manga, he is colored in pure black, pure white, and simple hatching for shadows. In the anime, he has simple base color with one layer of highlight, and one layer of shadow. A second chibi style also provides comic relief.

Annotation Studios

Fullmetal Alchemist (2003) was co-produced by Bones, a japanese animation studio, and Aniplex, a Sony subsidiary. The production was overseen by Seiji Mizushima, a japanese anime director responsible for many unique movies and shows. Hiromu Arakawa, the male pen name for Hiromi Arakawa, was the artist illustrating the manga which was still ongoing during the timeframe of the animation production. She wanted Bones studio to create a different ending from the one she was in the process of illustrating so she helped Bones create unique stories, personalities and plots for the original story.

Author

Hiromi Arakawa grew up on a rural dairy farm but always wanted to become a manga artist. During her childhood, most stories created by female artists were meant for girls and were incredibly “girly” while Arakawa was rather far more interested in deeper and darker plots that were typically meant for boys and written by almost exclusively male authors. With hard work and determination she created Full Metal Alchemist.

Timeline

The first iteration Fullmetal Alchemist came out in August 2003 and was updated monthly. Fullmetal Alchemsit Brotherhood came out in 2009 and was updated weekly until its finale in June 2010 which coincided with the ending of the manga series.

Different Permuations

Fullmetal Alchemist started as a manga by Arakawa, before being turned into Fullmetal Alchemist the animated series which ran at the same time as the manga so it had a different ending. Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood was the final version of the story, remaining faithful to the source material.

Company Ownership

The series is owned by Square Enix, a manga publishing company. The series has ended so there are no official animations or manga chapters. Enix now markets Fullmetal Alchemist as it is one of their best selling mangas. They have partnered with several other companies to make merchandise and follow up movies for example, Netflix’s adaptation into live action.

More about Studio Bones

The final series, Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood, was also produced by Bones, the same japanese production studio that produced the original series. Bones is broken into sub studios, where Fullmetal Alchemist was produced in Studio D and was directed by Yasuhiro Irie.

Voice Actor

Vic Mignognia is the voice actor for Edward Elric and was present in both the original and Brotherhood editions. He is also known for being in the english dub of Ouran Highschool Host Club. Edward Elric is voiced by Romi Park throughout the Fullmetal Alchemist Franchise. He is famous and is a voice actor in lots of animes including Naruto, Attack on Titan, and branching out to western dubs including Kung Fu Panda and Cars

Video Games

Edward Elric is in 3 specific Fullmetal Alchemist role play video games that were written and initially drawn by Arakawa before Studio Bones finished the animated scenes. Edward Elric also appears in several other video games as a collective character for example in MapleStory and Kingdom of Hearts.
